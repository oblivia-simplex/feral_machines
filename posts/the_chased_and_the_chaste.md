# The Chased and the Chaste

I want to understand the fear and disgust that the figure of the chaser elicits from
so many transsexual women. How much of it comes from experience, and how much from
innocence? There is, of course, something potent, something dangerous in male sexuality
-- and the fear of this *something*, all the more so. [AWK?]

The reasons that issue from experience seem clear enough, and I don't want to belittle
the role played by trauma, or by the cruelty performed by stigmatized, shamefaced men. 
In this respect, the situation of the tranny chaser isn't terribly different from that
of the chubby chaser, or any situation where one's object choice is marked by a steep
social gradient. These are the effects of asymmetrically stigmatized sex, and we find
them everywhere.

But I'm not sure that this exhausts the issue.

I wonder, sometimes, how much of the fear of chasers, especially among trans lesbians
(among, I mean, those whose pre-transition lives were essentially heterosexual) is a kind
of vestigal homophobia. 

But the women who hate the chaser don't hate him as a faggot, exactly -- and this is what
complicates things. The chaser, they'll tell you, is het -- he's het, of course, because
trans women are women, and there's nothing *other* than heterosexuality involved in fucking
us. And so, if he's a specialist, then his fixation is something *unsanctified* by orientation,
unlicensed by identity. It's a "fetish", a paraphilia, an obsession that reduces us to 
idiotic objects. 

There is something potent, something dangerous in male sexuality, and the fear of this 
*something*, of its deviations, is at the root of the most vicious forms of transmisogyny.
At the root, too, perhaps, of certain forms of dysphoria. I think that there's a fear that
that, that spark, is what attracts the chaser's attention. A desire that's unbearable 
because it's a desire aimed at *something* the desired wishes herself rid of. A desire that
marks her as *other* than a cisgender woman. 

The chaser is a reminder of our difference, as sexual beings, as objects of a desire at odds
with our own.

The fear and hatred of chasers -- by the straights and by trans women themselves -- is 
of a piece with transmisogny. The object of the chaser's lust is nothing other than that
*something* that marks us as different, that refuses our assimilation into the weave of
"natural" womanhood.
