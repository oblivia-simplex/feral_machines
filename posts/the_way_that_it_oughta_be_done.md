# The Way that it Ought to be Done

I want to write something about love and virtue.


I've been thinking, every day since I first read it, what Jackie
said about pessimism, betrayal, and uprightness:

> We are slack because we are disengaged from the current order, we
> are disengaged because we condemn that order, we condemn that order
> because we have some undeveloped kernel of an idea of a different
> order, that it could be otherwise, and this goes down into the
> feeling of betrayal. 

> what is needed, actually, is to bring ourselves up to the level of
> the vision we no longer dare to assert, perhaps which we dismissed
> as childish, etc. And it was childish, because we were children 
> when we thought of it and by adulthood largely stopped developing 
> it. 

> Here one has the example of the "mother tongue" -- many of us had
> a bit of a foreign language as children, but we are illiterate
> and incompetent in that language, because as we grew, we grew only
> as English speakers.
>
> Freedom is a language, and we speak it childishly, like a language
> where we never habitually communicated, never read a novel, never
> savored eloquence.

I don't think there's a better way to understand virtue than as the cultivation
of that language.

I often find myself thinking about a remark that Abelson (or Sussman?) made at
some point during their 1986 seminars on "The Structure and Interpretation of
Computer Programs": when we're approaching a new problem, the best thing to do
is often *not* to try to tackle the problem directly, or even piece-by-piece,
but to first develop a sort of domain-specific language in which that *kind* of
problem can be stated simply, and solved naturally. When we approach ethical
problems in this way, what we're contemplating, I think, is the cultivation of
virtue. 


Virtue is that without which ethics is not even legible, and freedom speaks
only in barks and squeals.


maybe a part of what we called indiscipline or moral incoherence -- the part that's not *just* garden variety akrasia -- is a matter of not living a language adequate to our ideals.
